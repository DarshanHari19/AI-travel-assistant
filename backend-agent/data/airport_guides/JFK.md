# John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK) - Strategic Intelligence

## Executive Summary

JFK is New York's primary international gateway, handling 62 million+ passengers annually across 6 terminals. Critical for business travelers: Terminal transfers can take 30-45 minutes due to AirTrain connections, and TSA wait times vary dramatically by terminal and time of day.

**Quick Stats:**
- **IATA Code:** JFK
- **Location:** Queens, New York (15 miles from Manhattan)
- **Terminals:** 6 active terminals (Terminal 2 closed for renovations through 2026)
- **Peak Hours:** 6:00-9:00 AM, 4:00-8:00 PM
- **Average Security Wait:** 20-45 minutes (60-90 minutes during peak)
- **Recommended Arrival:** 2.5 hours domestic, 3 hours international

## Terminal-by-Terminal Intelligence

### Terminal 1 - International Hub
**Airlines:** Air France, Japan Airlines, Korean Air, Lufthansa, Turkish Airlines

**Security Intelligence:**
- **Average Wait:** 25-40 minutes
- **Peak Times:** 4:00-7:00 PM (European departures)
- **TSA PreCheck:** Available (2 lanes)
- **CLEAR:** Available

**Business Traveler Tips:**
- Plaza Premium Lounge (pay-per-use, $50-65) offers showers and quiet workspaces
- Free WiFi throughout terminal (JFK-Free WiFi, no login required)
- Power outlets abundant at gates 1-6
- Best food: Deep Blue Sushi (Gate 1), Croque Madame (Gate 4)
- Currency exchange: Travelex (post-security, poor rates - use ATM instead)

**Critical Timing:**
- Check-in closes 60 minutes before departure (international)
- Gate closing: 20 minutes before departure
- Walking time from security to farthest gate: 12-15 minutes

---

### Terminal 4 - Delta Hub & International Mega-Terminal
**Airlines:** Delta (domestic & international), 30+ international carriers including Emirates, Etihad, Virgin Atlantic, KLM, Air India

**Security Intelligence:**
- **Average Wait:** 30-50 minutes (busiest terminal at JFK)
- **Peak Times:** 5:00-9:00 AM (morning departures), 5:00-8:00 PM (evening intl flights)
- **TSA PreCheck:** 6 lanes available (Gates B20-B40 area)
- **CLEAR:** 4 enrollment/scan stations

**Terminal Layout:**
- **Concourse A:** Gates A1-A9 (Delta domestic)
- **Concourse B:** Gates B20-B48 (Delta & international)
- Terminal is MASSIVE - allow 20 minutes to walk from Concourse A to far end of B

**Business Traveler Intelligence:**
- **Delta Sky Club:** 3 locations (best: Gate B41, largest with showers)
- **Wingtips Lounge:** Premium offering with lie-flat chairs (Gate B37)
- **Virgin Atlantic Clubhouse:** Exceptional lounge with spa, cocktail bar (Gate B38, Virgin-ticketed passengers only)
- **Work-Friendly Spots:** Seats near Gate B30 have individual power + USB
- **Food Recommendations:** 
  - Shake Shack (Concourse A, expect 15-min wait)
  - Chefs' Hall food court (Concourse B, pre-security, excellent variety)
  - Fresh&Co (healthy options, quick service)

**Transfer Intelligence:**
- **Domestic to International within T4:** 15-25 min walk, no re-security
- **International arrivals:** Immigration takes 30-90 min (mobile passport app helps)
- **Customs to re-check bags:** Add 20-30 minutes for connections

**Critical Business Traveler Notes:**
- Terminal 4 has dedicated FedEx/UPS shipping (post-security, Concourse B)
- Conference rooms available in Delta Sky Club (reservation required)
- SIM card vending machines (Concourse B, near Gate B35)
- Embassy Suites connected via Skywalk (5 min walk, dayrooms sometimes available)

### Terminal 5 - JetBlue Exclusive Haven
**Airlines:** JetBlue (exclusive terminal)

**Security Intelligence:**
- **Average Wait:** 15-30 minutes (best-managed security at JFK)
- **Peak Times:** 6:00-8:00 AM, 5:00-7:00 PM
- **TSA PreCheck:** 3 dedicated lanes
- **CLEAR:** Available

**Why Business Travelers Love T5:**
- Modern, spacious design (opened 2008, renovated 2020)
- Fastest security processing at JFK
- Consistent WiFi speeds (50+ Mbps)
- Best food options of any JFK terminal

**Food & Amenities:**
- **Shake Shack:** Near Gate 10 (lunch/dinner crowds 12-2pm, 5-7pm)
- **Deep Blue Sushi:** Gate 14 (excellent quality for airport)
- **Loft Food Hall:** Rotating local NYC vendors
- **Multifaith Prayer Room:** Quiet space between Gates 20-25
- **Rooftop Terrace:** Gates 13-14 (outdoor space, rare at airports)

**JetBlue Mint Business Class Perks:**
- Private suite check-in (skip main queues entirely)
- Dedicated security lane (3-5 minute wait typical)
- Mint Lounge with a la carte dining (not buffet)

**Connection Intelligence:**
- All gates walkable in 10-15 minutes max
- No AirTrain needed (unlike inter-terminal connections)
- Minimum connection time: 45 minutes domestic, 90 minutes international

### Terminal 8 - American Airlines Flagship
**Airlines:** American Airlines, British Airways (some flights), Qatar Airways

**Security Intelligence:**
- **Average Wait:** 25-40 minutes
- **Peak Times:** 6:00-9:00 AM (morning departures), 5:00-8:00 PM (evening)

**Layout:**
- Concourse: Gates 10-59 in one long corridor
- Walking time end-to-end: 18-20 minutes

**Critical for Connections:**
- **AA to AA domestic:** Minimum 45 min
- **AA to International partner:** Minimum 90 min (BA, Qatar connect here)
- **International arrival to domestic departure:** Minimum 2 hours (immigration + re-check bags)

## Inter-Terminal Transfers

### AirTrain JFK (Free Between Terminals)
**Transfer Times (Terminal to Terminal):**
- T1 ↔ T4: 8-12 minutes
- T4 ↔ T5: 5-8 minutes
- T5 ↔ T7: 7-10 minutes
- T5 ↔ T8: 4-6 minutes
- T1 ↔ T8: 15-20 minutes (longest route)

**Critical Timing Rules:**
- Add 10-15 minutes for walking to/from AirTrain station within each terminal
- AirTrain runs every 4-8 minutes (6 AM - 11 PM), every 10-15 min (11 PM - 6 AM)
- Total inter-terminal transfer: **Budget 30-45 minutes minimum**
- During irregular operations (delays/cancellations): Can take 60+ min due to crowds

**Business Traveler Hack:**
- If transferring T5 → T8 or vice versa, consider walking via parking garage connector (20 min walk, avoids AirTrain crowds)

## Ground Transportation to Manhattan

### For Business Travelers - Speed & Reliability Ranked

#### 1. **LIRR (Long Island Rail Road) + AirTrain - FASTEST**
- **Total Time to Midtown:** 35-45 minutes
- **Cost:** $13.25 total ($8.25 AirTrain to Jamaica Station + $5-7.25 LIRR to Penn Station)
- **Frequency:** Every 15-30 min during peak, every 30-60 min off-peak
- **Best For:** Meetings in Midtown (34th St area), travelers with carry-on only
- **Avoid if:** You have large luggage, going to Lower Manhattan, traveling late night (after 11 PM)

**Pro Tips:**
- Use LIRR app to check schedule before leaving terminal
- Jamaica Station has escalators but crowded (allow 10 min to navigate)
- Penn Station has direct subway access to all Manhattan

#### 2. **Uber/Lyft Black - MOST CONVENIENT**
- **Time to Midtown:** 45-75 minutes (traffic-dependent)
- **Cost:** $75-120 (varies by demand/time)
- **Best For:** Multiple people, luggage, door-to-door service
- **Worst Times:** 7-10 AM, 4-7 PM (can take 90+ min)

**Strategic Timing:**
- Best times: 10 AM-3 PM, after 8 PM
- Avoid Friday 3-8 PM (worst traffic of week)

#### 3. **Yellow Cab - FLAT RATE PREDICTABILITY**
- **Time to Manhattan:** 45-75 minutes
- **Cost:** **$70 flat rate** + tolls (~$7) + tip (15-20%) = ~$90 total
- **Best For:** Expense reports (flat rate easy to document), late night (always available)
- **Note:** Flat rate only applies to Manhattan destinations (not Brooklyn, Queens, etc.)

#### 4. **Airport Shuttle/Bus - BUDGET OPTION**
- **NYC Airporter:** $19-25, runs every 30 min to Grand Central/Penn Station/Port Authority
- **Time:** 60-90 minutes (multiple stops)
- **Best For:** Solo travelers on budget, non-time-sensitive
- **Avoid for:** Business meetings with tight timing

### Reverse: Manhattan to JFK

**Critical Flight Departure Time Planning:**
- **Morning Departures (6-9 AM):** Leave Manhattan by 4:00-4:30 AM
- **Midday Departures (10 AM-2 PM):** Leave Manhattan 2.5-3 hours before flight
- **Evening Departures (5-9 PM):** Leave Manhattan 3-3.5 hours before (rush hour traffic)

**Friday & Sunday Caution:** Add 30-60 minutes to all estimates (peak travel days)

## Time-of-Day Security Intelligence

### Best Times to Clear Security (Minimal Wait)
- **Early:** 5:00-6:00 AM (before morning rush)
- **Mid-Morning:** 10:00 AM-12:00 PM
- **Off-Peak:** 2:00-4:00 PM

### Worst Times (60-90 Minute Waits Possible)
- **Morning Peak:** 6:30-9:00 AM
- **Evening Peak:** 5:00-8:00 PM
- **Sunday evenings:** 4:00-9:00 PM (returning business travelers)

### Terminal -Specific Patterns
- **Terminal 5 (JetBlue):** Most consistent, rarely exceeds 30 min even in peak
- **Terminal 4:** Major variability - can be 20 min at 2 PM, 75 min at 6 PM
- **Terminal 8:** Mornings slower than evenings (counterintuitive - morning int'l departures)

## Business Traveler Survival Guide

### Pre-Security (Landside)
**Where to Meet Clients/Colleagues:**
- **Terminal 4:** Chefs' Hall (public area, good coffee, quiet morning hours)
- **Terminal 5:** Arrival level near baggage claim (open seating area)
- **Terminal 8:** Admirals Club (if both have access)

**Last-Minute Needs:**
- **Printing/FedEx:** Terminal 4 (post-security, Concourse B), Terminal 8 (Admirals Club)
- **Pharmacy:** Terminal 4 has CVS (pre-security)
- **Business Attire:** Men's Wearhouse Express (Terminal 4 pre-security) - emergency suits, ties

### Post-Security (Airside)
**Where to Work:**
- **Best WiFi:** Terminal 5 (fastest, most reliable), Terminal 8
- **Quietest Spots:** Terminal 1 (far gates 1-3), Terminal 5 (rooftop terrace), T8 (Five Senses Spa waiting area)
- **Power Outlets:** Abundant in all terminals - look for newer gate areas

**Conference Calls:**
- **Private Spaces:** Delta Sky Club private rooms (T4), BA First Lounge (T7), Admirals Flagship (T8)
- **Quiet Public Spots:** T5 rooftop, T1 far gates, T4 near Gate B48 (quieter end)
- **Avoid:** All food courts, gate areas 15 min before boarding

### Irregular Operations (Delays/Cancellations)
**Best Terminals to Get Stuck In:**
1. **Terminal 5:** Best food, comfortable seating, good WiFi
2. **Terminal 8:** Lounges, spa, food variety
3. **Terminal 4:** Size means more options, but gets crowded

**Strategic Moves During IROPS:**
- Gate agents are slammed - use airline app to rebook (faster)
- If connection missed, head to lounge or customer service (not gate)
- Hotel vouchers: Ask specifically (not automatic), best luck at AA/Delta counters
- Terminal 5 has outdoor terrace (get fresh air during long waits)

### Executive Traveler Hacks
1. **Mobile Passport App:** Saves 20-40 min on international arrivals (T4, T1)
2. **Reserve TSA PreCheck Queue:** Some airlines allow appointment booking (AA, Delta)
3. **Lounge Day Passes:** Buy online in advance (cheaper than at desk) - LoungeBuddy app
4. **Terminal 4 → 5 Walk:** If transferring with >90 min, walk parking garage route to T5 for better food
5. **Late Night Arrivals:** If landing after 11 PM, LIRR service sparse - pre-book car service
6. **JFK WiFi Pro Tip:** "JFK-Free WiFi" works in terminals, but slow. Use cellular hotspot for calls/uploads

## Seasonal & Weather Considerations

### Winter (Dec-Mar)
- **Delays Common:** Snow/ice causes 30-60 min delays (de-icing)
- **Security Slower:** Passengers wearing bulky coats
- **Best Practice:** Arrive 30 min earlier than usual during snow advisories
- **Ground Transport:** Allow extra 30-45 min for road conditions

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- **Thunderstorms:** Afternoon/evening (3-8 PM) delays common
- **Crowds:** Peak tourist season + business travel = longest security waits
- **AC Issues:** Terminal 1 and 7 can get warm during extreme heat

### Holiday Periods (Thanksgiving, Christmas)
- **Security Waits:** 90-120 minutes possible at Terminal 4
- **Recommendation:** Arrive 3.5-4 hours early for domestic (!), 4+ hours international
- **Lounges:** At capacity - arrive early if relying on lounge access
