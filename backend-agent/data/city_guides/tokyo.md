# Strategic Travel Guide: Tokyo

## Seasonal Climate Patterns

### Summer (June - August)
* **June (Tsuyu/Early Summer):** The rainy season begins. Expect constant light-to-medium precipitation. Humidity is high (75-85%), but wind is low. Average temperatures: 21-26°C (70-79°F).
* **July - August (Peak Summer):** Hot and extremely humid conditions. Temperatures typically range 26-32°C (79-90°F) with humidity often exceeding 80%. Brief afternoon thunderstorms are common. Heat index can feel significantly higher due to urban heat island effect.
* **Summer Business Travel Tips:** Indoor venues are heavily air-conditioned (often 20-22°C), so bring a light jacket. Outdoor meetings are rare during peak summer. Stay hydrated and use covered walkways when possible in business districts.

### Fall Transition
* **September - October:** Typhoon season. High probability of significant flight diversions from Narita (NRT) to Haneda (HND) or Osaka. Temperature cools to 20-27°C (68-81°F) but humidity remains elevated through September.

## Business Intelligence
* **Airport Strategy:** Haneda is significantly closer to the city center. In the event of a Typhoon warning, NRT often shuts down rail links (Narita Express) earlier than HND, potentially stranding travelers.
* **Etiquette:** It is expected to carry a "compact umbrella" (folding) for business meetings. Appearing at a high-level meeting drenched is considered a lack of preparation.
